## 1. Brand Overview

**Category:** DTC dog supplements
**Format:** Physical product, multiple SKUs
**Device priority:** Mobile-first
**Heritage:** Family laboratory doing animal functional nutrition since the 1960s — before pet supplements existed as a category
**Origin:** Made in Italy, to pharmaceutical grade
**Founders:** Two co-founders, one dog

### Core brand point of view

Prevention, not reaction. The brand's position is that proactive daily nutrition is the standard of care — not a luxury, not a niche — and that most of the supplement category is built on the wrong premise (responding to symptoms rather than preventing them).

**Canine longevity framing:** Longevity doesn't start at the first vet visit. It starts on an ordinary Tuesday, before anything is wrong. The scientific premise: the daily inputs made during a dog's healthy years — not the interventions made after decline — are what determine healthspan. This is not an abstract idea; it is the central finding of long-run canine nutrition research.

**The daily ritual premise:** Health is not built in a single decision. It is built in a thousand small ones — one chew, one morning, repeated. The ritual is the product. Simplicity is the feature.

## 2. Product Architecture

Two distinct collections presented as a system with two entry points.

### Daily Foundation

- **Who it's for:** Dogs of all ages, especially younger dogs with no symptoms
- **The logic:** Build health before it needs defending. Daily nutrition as a ritual, not a remedy. One chew, same time each day — because habits that require no decision survive.
- **Hero line:** "Designed for the dogs who look perfectly fine."
- **The longevity chain:** Daily Foundation → sustained over time → immune readiness · antioxidant defence · steady energy metabolism → cumulative slowing of the ageing process → longer healthspan. Outcomes are structural, not immediate.
- **CTA:** Primary (filled button)

### Targeted Care

- **Who it's for:** Dogs showing specific signs, or proactive owners who don't want to wait
- **The logic:** Precise support for specific body systems. Entry point for older dogs or those with identified needs.
- **Hero line:** "Waiting for symptoms is not a strategy."
- **CTA:** Secondary (outlined button)

### System line

> **Foundation first. Then targeted care.**

The quiz ("Find my supplement") sits as a third path below both collections for undecided visitors.

## 4. Strategic Frame

### 4.1 The Three Jobs of the Homepage

Every section must serve one of three jobs — in this order:

1. **Stop the scroll** — the hero earns attention through differentiation, not product description. Abstract, emotional, and unexpected are all acceptable here.
2. **Land the concept** — within 2–3 sections, abstraction must connect to a specific product, ingredient, or scientific fact. The reader should never have to do the work of understanding what Broono actually is.
3. **Index the category** — the hero does not do SEO work. The first body section beneath it must contain functional, keyword-aware language. The glossary, blog, and product pages carry the long-term organic load.

### 4.2 The Central Category Claim

Broono is building a new category: **healthspan-first canine care**. This word — healthspan — is unclaimed in the dog supplement space. Every page should quietly normalise it.

**Site definition (use verbatim — optimised for GEO citation):**

> "Healthspan is the number of years your dog spends in good health — moving freely, thinking clearly, engaged with the world. It is distinct from lifespan. The gap between the two is what Broono exists to narrow."

### 4.3 The Abstraction Rule

Abstract heroes are intentional. The failure mode is not opening abstractly — it is staying abstract too long.

- **Hero:** Emotional, arresting, unexpected. No product mention required.
- **First body section:** Still conceptual but now specific. A real fact, a real number, a real research finding.
- **By section 3:** Product named. Ingredients real. Doses declared.

### 4.4 The Sage Voice

Broono's archetype is The Sage — knowledge-based authority, calm confidence, mentor not salesperson.

The Sage's authority comes from what it knows, not from what others don't. It does not position through comparison, criticism, or contrast. It simply states what is true, clearly, and lets the reader draw the conclusion. The Sage trusts the reader's intelligence — it never oversimplifies, never condescends, and never needs to alarm.

**The four operating principles:**

1. **Emotion in headlines. Clarity in body copy.** Poetic outside. Rational inside. The headline earns attention; the body copy earns trust. These are different jobs and they require different registers. Do not write emotional body copy or clinical headlines.
2. **Never stay in the emotional register long enough to manipulate.** The Sage uses emotion as a door, not a room. Open with feeling; move quickly to knowledge, agency, and fact. Any section that stays emotional for more than one sentence without pivoting to something concrete has crossed into Caregiver or Manipulator territory.
3. **Authority through knowledge, not contrast.** The Sage never needs to say "unlike others" or "most brands do X." That is the Rebel archetype. If Broono's formulation is better, the Sage demonstrates it — with a dose, a mechanism, a cited study. The implicit contrast is always more powerful than the stated one.
4. **Empower, never alarm.** The Sage creates agency. Every piece of copy should leave the reader feeling more capable, more informed, more certain of what to do next — not behind, not guilty, not scared. The question to ask before publishing anything: *does this create agency or anxiety?*

**The three recurring failure modes — flag and fix on sight:**

- **Rebel drift:** Copy that positions Broono by diminishing the category (*"most supplements react," "most brands ignore"*). Delete the comparison. State the Broono truth directly.
- **Guilt framing:** Copy that implies the reader is currently falling short (*"you don't wait to eat well, neither should they," "have you been watching closely enough?"*). Reframe around what the reader *can* do, not what they haven't done.
- **Urgency without agency:** Copy that creates a sense of time pressure without immediately offering a concrete action. *"It may already be too late"* is the extreme version. Subtler forms include any sentence that names a problem in the final position — the last word of a section should always be a solution or a step forward, never a threat.

**The test:** *Does this feel like a house built around devotion — or a company selling chews?*

**Notes:**

## 8. SEO & GEO Strategy

### 8.1 What the Hero Cannot Do

Abstract heroes do not rank. "No other animal on Earth evolved specifically to understand you" contains zero search-intent language. This is by design — do not try to optimise the hero for SEO. It will destroy both the copy and the rankings.

### 8.2 Where the SEO Load Lives

| Page zone                    | SEO/GEO job                                                                                            |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Hero                         | None. Brand identity only.                                                                             |
| First body section (The Gap) | Category entry — "healthspan," "preventative care for dogs," "daily dog supplement"                   |
| Product sections             | Benefit language, product names, ingredient names                                                      |
| Ingredient Glossary          | Highest-value GEO asset — answers "what does [ingredient] do for dogs?"                               |
| Blog                         | Long-form topical authority — "how to support dog joint health," "canine cognitive dysfunction," etc. |
| Product page body copy       | Long-tail SEO — specific ingredient names, dosages, benefit areas                                     |

### 8.3 The Healthspan Opportunity

Nobody owns "healthspan" in the dog supplement space. Broono should. Specific actions:

- Use "healthspan" in the first body section of the homepage
- Use it in the H1 of the Ingredient Glossary page
- Use it in blog article titles
- Use it in meta descriptions across product pages
- The site definition in §4.2 is formatted for AI answer engine citation — use it verbatim

### 8.4 GEO (AI Answer Engines)

The Ingredient Glossary is the single highest-value GEO asset. Write each ingredient entry as if answering: "what does [ingredient] do for dogs?" in plain language, with a sourced claim. This is the format that gets cited.

## 10. Voice Guardrails

### 10.1 The Agency Test

Before publishing any copy: does this create **agency** or **anxiety**? Any copy that makes the reader feel behind, guilty, scared, or helpless has drifted off-brand — even if it would convert. The long-term cost to trust is not worth the short-term conversion lift.

### 10.2 The Abstraction Landing Strip

Every abstract opening must have a landing strip — a deliberate path from poetic to precise: Abstract → specific fact → product. If you cannot draw that line clearly, the section is not ready.

### 10.3 What We Never Do

- Countdown timers, fake scarcity, urgency tactics
- "Before it's too late" framing
- Sick-dog imagery or dramatic decline narratives
- Miracle claims or cure language
- Superlatives ("best," "most powerful," "#1")
- Exclamation marks in body copy
- Emoji in any copy
- "Proprietary blend" or vague formulation language
- Guilt-based copy ("Don't you want the best for your dog?")

### 10.4 The Quality Check

Every piece of copy should pass this test before it publishes:

> *Does this feel like a house built around devotion — or a company selling chews?*

### 14.1 — PDP Design Principles

The PDP visitor has intent. They arrived from a product card, collection page, or ad. The page does not need to introduce Broono — it needs to confirm the visitor is in the right place, educate them on this specific product, and remove every remaining doubt before the action.

**The three jobs of the PDP — in order:**

1. **Confirm the decision** — above the fold, the visitor should immediately know: right product, right collection, right brand.
2. **Earn the purchase through transparency** — ingredients named, doses declared, mechanisms sourced. The science section carries the formulation rationale. No vague claims.
3. **Remove the last doubt** — reviews and FAQ are not decoration. They are the final conversation before the purchase.

**PDP-specific voice note:** The Sage voice on the PDP operates at closer range than on the homepage. The visitor is not browsing — they are evaluating. Copy should be more precise, less poetic. Emotion in headlines; clarity in every line of body copy. The abstraction rule still applies, but the landing strip is shorter.

**Colour:** Each PDP uses the product's secondary colour as the accent throughout — collection badge, pull-quote, ingredient callout borders, science section background tints.

**Subscription snippet — Layout H (confirmed wireframe default):**

The subscription widget uses a two-section card pattern (wireframe Layout H):

- **Section 2 — Frequency:** Three stacked selectable cards, radio-button style, black border on active. Content updates dynamically per weight band:

| Weight band | Freq 1 | Freq 2 | Freq 3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Up to 10 kg | 1 jar · every 60 days | 2 jars · every 120 days | 3 jars · every 180 days |
| 10–25 kg | 1 jar · every 30 days | 2 jars · every 60 days | 3 jars · every 90 days |
| 25 kg+ | 1 jar · every 20 days | 2 jars · every 40 days | 3 jars · every 60 days |

Each frequency card shows: supply name + interval · Save X% badge · **£X.XX/day** + ~~barred £X.XX/day~~.

Total shipment price (discounted) + struck-through full price shown above the CTA, not inside cells.

**Subscription pricing logic:**

- Jar price: **£22.90**
- Discount tiers: Freq 1 = **10% off**, Freq 2 = **15% off**, Freq 3 = **20% off**
- Discounted total = jars × £22.90 × (1 − discount%)
- Full price (struck-through, muted) = jars × £22.90
- Price per day = discounted total ÷ delivery interval in days
- Barred price per day (struck-through) = full price ÷ delivery interval in days
- Currency: GBP (£)
