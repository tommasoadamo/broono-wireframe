# Broono CMO Brief: Traffic Generation And Performance Marketing Discussion

> Prepared for: CMO prep before discussion with AI/performance marketing operator  
> Date: 2026-05-07  
> Purpose: Align Broono's thinking before an open conversation on Meta and other traffic channels.

---

## 1. The Core Question

Broono should use the call to explore one central question:

**How should Broono build a traffic system that creates demand, educates the buyer, and converts profitably without becoming a generic performance-marketing supplement brand?**

This is not an agency evaluation call. The objective is not to decide whether to hire this person or their team. The objective is to extract strategic insight from someone who has seen a supplement/wellness brand scale aggressively, including through paid channels, creative testing, product habit formation, and broader demand generation.

The person we are speaking with reportedly helped Gruns scale into a billion-dollar outcome. Public reporting in April 2026 indicates that Unilever acquired Gruns for an estimated $1.2B. Gruns is a useful reference point because it appears to have combined digital demand creation, high-frequency product usage, strong product experience, broad audience appeal, retail expansion, and disciplined omnichannel growth. Broono should learn from that trajectory without copying it. Gruns is a human supplement brand with a mass-market format. Broono is a premium canine healthspan brand with a science-led, trust-first position. The mechanics may transfer. The voice and category strategy should not be imported blindly.

The call should help Broono understand:

- What Meta can realistically do for a new premium pet supplement brand.
- Which channels create demand versus capture demand.
- How much education is required before conversion.
- What creative formats are likely to work without damaging trust.
- What economics must be true for paid acquisition to scale.
- How to sequence paid traffic, organic authority, email, creator content, and website conversion.
- How to learn from Gruns' growth model while preserving Broono's own brand logic.

## 2. Where Broono Is Now

Broono is at a strategically important transition point. The brand is not starting from zero, but it is also not yet in full growth mode. The current phase is best understood as **pre-scale foundation building**.

### Brand And Positioning

Broono is being repositioned as a **modern healthspan house for dogs**. The brand is not trying to compete as a generic dog supplement, a symptom-solution product, or an Amazon-style pet wellness SKU. The core positioning is prevention, daily ritual, and long-term biological support.

The current strategic point of view:

- Dog care should begin before something goes wrong.
- Healthspan, not just lifespan, is the category idea Broono should own.
- The product is a daily ritual, not a reactive intervention.
- Broono should feel like a premium wellness brand that happens to be for dogs, not a pet-store supplement brand.
- Trust should be earned through formulation transparency, scientific clarity, ingredient logic, and calm authority.

This creates a high bar for performance marketing. The brand cannot rely on fear, urgency, miracle claims, before/after drama, fake scarcity, or cheap direct-response tropes. Broono has to sell the idea of prevention to people whose dogs may currently look healthy.

### Product

The product has been tested and appears viable. This is critical because paid traffic can expose weak product-market fit very quickly. Broono is not using Meta to discover whether people care about dog wellness at all; the more useful question is which audience, message, offer, and funnel architecture can scale demand profitably.

The product architecture includes daily-use soft chews across benefit areas such as foundation/longevity, movement, digestion, and calm. The strongest strategic wedge appears to be **Daily Foundation**: a baseline ritual for dogs who look perfectly fine. Targeted products can serve as clearer entry points for existing needs, but the larger brand opportunity is to normalize preventative canine healthspan care.

### Website

Broono is currently building the new website. The site is designed to do more than transact. It needs to educate, establish trust, explain the category, and support paid and organic traffic.

The website strategy currently includes:

- A homepage that introduces the brand and product system.
- Product pages that explain mechanisms, ingredients, doses, and trust signals.
- An ingredient glossary designed as a major SEO/GEO asset.
- Journal/blog content intended to build authority around canine healthspan.
- Quiz and email capture pathways for visitors who are not ready to buy immediately.

This matters because paid media should not be evaluated separately from the landing experience. If Meta traffic fails, the reason may be creative, audience, offer, page, education gap, trust gap, or unit economics. The new site should be treated as part of the acquisition system, not as a separate brand project.

### Content And Creative Assets

Broono has an Instagram calendar ready, and new shoots are ongoing. This is a strong moment to ask how content production should serve both organic and paid channels.

Key question: should Broono produce content primarily for brand world-building, paid testing, education, founder authority, product explanation, or all of the above?

The shoot plan should be pressure-tested against performance needs. Paid channels often require more creative volume and more message variation than brand teams expect. But Broono should not produce generic UGC or low-trust social creative simply because it performs elsewhere. The creative system needs to support test velocity while remaining recognizably Broono.

### Growth Readiness

Broono is close to the moment where traffic generation becomes useful, but it should avoid premature scaling. The immediate goal should be learning: identifying which traffic sources, angles, landing pages, and offers produce qualified intent.

Before scaling spend, Broono should clarify:

- The target CAC range that could work.
- Gross margin and contribution margin by product and bundle.
- Expected repeat purchase behavior.
- Subscription or reorder strategy.
- AOV targets and bundle architecture.
- Email/SMS capture strategy.
- Landing page hierarchy for cold, warm, and high-intent traffic.
- Claims review process for paid ads and landing pages.

The question is not "Can we buy traffic?" The question is "Can we build a system where each dollar of traffic teaches us something and compounds into a stronger brand?"

## 3. How Broono Should Think About Performance Marketing

Performance marketing should not be treated as a channel team buying conversions. For Broono, it should be a structured learning system across four jobs:

1. **Create demand** among people who do not yet know they need preventative dog healthspan care.
2. **Educate the buyer** so the product makes sense before price becomes the main comparison point.
3. **Capture existing intent** from people already searching for dog supplements, joint support, digestion support, calming chews, longevity, or premium pet wellness.
4. **Convert and retain** through a website, email, subscription/reorder flow, and product experience that make daily use habitual.

### Demand Creation Versus Demand Capture

Meta, TikTok, creators, podcasts, newsletters, and organic social are primarily demand-creation channels. They interrupt people before they are actively shopping. They need a sharp reason to care.

Google Search, Shopping, SEO, comparison content, and high-intent landing pages are demand-capture channels. They convert people who already know the problem or category.

Broono's challenge is that its biggest idea, healthspan-first canine care, is likely not yet a high-volume search behavior. People may search for "dog joint supplement" or "dog probiotics," but fewer will search for "dog healthspan supplement." Therefore, Broono needs both:

- Demand capture around familiar needs.
- Demand creation around the larger healthspan idea.

The strategic risk is over-investing in bottom-of-funnel problem language and becoming just another "hips/gut/calm" brand. The commercial risk is over-investing in abstract brand education and failing to convert. Broono needs a bridge: healthspan as the brand idea, specific body systems and products as the buying path.

### Meta's Likely Role

Meta should probably be treated as a creative and message testing engine first, and a scale engine second. It can help Broono learn which ideas make cold audiences stop, understand, and act.

Potential Meta roles:

- Test positioning angles.
- Build remarketing pools.
- Drive email capture or quiz completion.
- Send warm audiences to PDPs or bundles.
- Retarget people who engaged with educational content.
- Validate creative formats before larger spend.

Meta should not be expected to solve unclear positioning, weak landing pages, insufficient product education, or poor economics. If the economics do not support paid acquisition, Meta will reveal that quickly.

### The Funnel Question

Broono should not assume every click goes straight to a PDP. A cold audience may need an educational bridge before purchase, especially for the Daily Foundation concept.

Possible landing paths to discuss:

- **Direct PDP:** Best for high-intent targeted products or strong product-led creative.
- **Collection/product system page:** Useful when explaining Foundation versus Targeted Care.
- **Quiz:** Useful if the buyer does not know what their dog needs.
- **Advertorial/education page:** Useful for cold audiences who need to understand prevention or healthspan first.
- **Ingredient/science page:** Useful for trust-building retargeting or SEO/GEO traffic.
- **Homepage:** Best if the creative sells the brand world, but often too broad for paid acquisition.

The right answer may be multiple paths by audience temperature and message:

- Cold health-conscious dog owners: education or quiz.
- Symptom-aware dog owners: targeted PDP.
- Engaged social audience: product system page.
- Returning visitors: offer, bundle, or subscription page.
- Ingredient/science seekers: glossary or article to email capture.

### The Creative Question

Creative is likely the highest-leverage variable. For Broono, creative should test the buyer's mental model, not just ad formats.

Potential message territories:

- **Healthspan:** "The years spent well are the years worth protecting."
- **Daily ritual:** "One chew, same time each day, repeated."
- **Prevention:** "Built for the dog who looks perfectly fine."
- **Human standard:** "The standard you apply to your own supplements, applied to theirs."
- **Ingredient transparency:** "Every ingredient listed. Every dose declared."
- **Italian lab and craft:** "Made in Italy, with pharmaceutical-grade seriousness."
- **Founder/family story:** "A family nutrition lab brought into modern dog care."
- **Body systems:** "Support the systems that shape how a dog ages."

The creative guardrail: emotion can open the door, but the body of the message must create agency and trust. Broono should avoid fear-led hooks that imply the owner is failing their dog.

### The Economics Question

Before discussing scale, Broono needs to know what must be true for paid traffic to work.

The call should explore:

- Target CAC by product and by first-order bundle.
- Gross margin after COGS, fulfillment, payment fees, and discounts.
- First-order contribution margin.
- Expected reorder rate.
- Subscription attach rate.
- Payback period tolerance.
- AOV required to support paid acquisition.
- Whether bundles or starter kits are necessary.
- How much email capture can subsidize cold traffic.

If Broono has strong retention, it can tolerate higher first-order CAC. If reorder behavior is unproven, the brand should be more conservative and use smaller tests to learn.

### Working Budget Frame To Challenge

Broono should ask for the expert's view, but it is useful to enter the call with a starting hypothesis.

For Meta, there is a difference between three levels of spend:

- **Exploratory spend:** Enough to see early qualitative signals, such as click-through rate, hook resonance, landing page engagement, email capture rate, and add-to-cart behavior. This can help identify which messages deserve more testing, but it is usually not enough to judge CAC confidently.
- **Decision-grade testing spend:** Enough to test several creative concepts, generate meaningful conversion data, and compare landing paths. This is the level Broono should define before launch.
- **Scaling spend:** Spend only after creative, funnel, offer, and economics are directionally proven.

The call should pressure-test what these thresholds are. A useful answer may look something like:

- Conservative learning test: approximately 30 days, limited channels, limited creative concepts.
- Proper Meta test: approximately 60-90 days, enough budget to test multiple creative territories and landing paths.
- Scale phase: only after Broono has confidence in CAC, AOV, conversion rate, and repeat purchase assumptions.

The most important distinction: a very small budget may be useful for creative learning, but it should not be used to conclude that Meta "works" or "does not work."

## 4. What To Learn From The Gruns Playbook

The Gruns comparison is valuable because it scaled very quickly in supplements, but Broono should treat it as a case study, not a template.

Publicly visible elements of the Gruns growth story include:

- A simple, enjoyable daily format.
- Broad market relevance rather than a narrow clinical niche.
- Strong usage frequency and habit formation.
- Heavy digital demand creation.
- Omnichannel growth across DTC, Meta, Google, TikTok, influencers, and retail.
- Retail expansion that appears to have followed strong product and demand signals.
- A product proposition that can be understood quickly.

Questions to ask about their approach:

- What was the original insight that made Gruns scalable?
- Was growth driven more by product format, creative, offer, channel mix, or retention?
- What did Meta do specifically in the Gruns growth system?
- What did they learn from early failed tests?
- What creative angles scaled the most?
- How did they decide when to broaden the audience?
- How much education was needed before conversion?
- How did they balance DTC with retail?
- What metrics mattered most before the business had scale?
- What would they do differently if building a premium, trust-led pet supplement brand?

Most important: ask them to separate **transferable principles** from **Gruns-specific advantages**.

Transferable principles may include:

- Make the product easy to understand.
- Build around a daily habit.
- Use creative testing to find the language customers respond to.
- Let retention and usage frequency drive acquisition confidence.
- Sequence channels instead of treating them as isolated bets.
- Keep product experience central to marketing.

Gruns-specific advantages may include:

- Human supplement category familiarity.
- Gummy format novelty.
- Broad adult/kid/family relevance.
- Easier sampling and taste appeal.
- Large wellness market tailwinds.
- Potentially faster retail readability.

Broono's equivalent advantages may be different:

- Deep emotional bond between owner and dog.
- Preventative care as an underdeveloped pet category idea.
- Premium Italian lab and family heritage.
- Ingredient transparency in a low-trust supplement market.
- A specific white space around canine healthspan.
- Daily use that can become part of the feeding routine.

## 5. Recommended Call Structure

### Opening Frame

Set the tone clearly:

> "We are not here to evaluate an agency engagement today. We are trying to sharpen how Broono should think about traffic generation. We are rebranding, building the new website, preparing our organic calendar, shooting new assets, and validating a product we believe is viable. We want to understand how you would think about Meta and other channels for a premium dog healthspan brand, and what you learned from scaling Gruns that may or may not apply here."

### Topic 1: Diagnosis

Questions:

- What would you need to know before recommending any channel strategy?
- Based on what you know, what would you see as Broono's biggest growth constraint?
- Is this primarily a traffic problem, a positioning problem, a funnel problem, a creative problem, or an economics problem?
- What would make you cautious about scaling paid media too early?

### Topic 2: Channel Strategy

Questions:

- Which channels would you prioritize in the first 6 months?
- What role should Meta play: demand creation, creative testing, retargeting, conversion, or all of these?
- Where would Google Search, Shopping, TikTok, creators, SEO, email, and affiliates fit?
- Which channels create demand for a category like this, and which channels capture existing demand?
- What would you avoid doing at the beginning?

### Topic 3: Meta And Paid Social

Questions:

- How would you structure the first Meta tests?
- What budget is needed to get useful signal?
- Would you optimize first for purchases, email signups, quiz completions, add-to-cart, or content engagement?
- Would you start broad, interest-based, lookalike, Advantage+, or a mix?
- How long should Broono test before deciding whether Meta is working?
- How do you distinguish bad creative from bad audience, bad landing page, or bad economics?

### Topic 4: Creative And Messaging

Questions:

- What creative angles would you test first for Broono?
- How would you explain healthspan quickly in paid social?
- Would you lead with prevention, daily ritual, ingredient transparency, Italian lab quality, founder story, or specific symptoms?
- What paid social formats work best now for supplements?
- What role should founder-led video play?
- What type of creator content would be credible for this category?
- How do we generate urgency without fear, guilt, or fake scarcity?

### Topic 5: Funnel And Website

Questions:

- Where should cold Meta traffic land?
- When should we send traffic to a PDP versus a quiz, advertorial, homepage, collection page, or education page?
- What does the minimum viable paid landing page need to include?
- What trust signals matter most before purchase?
- How much scientific explanation helps conversion, and when does it become too much?
- What should we have live before spending meaningfully?

### Topic 6: Economics And Scaling

Questions:

- What CAC range would be realistic for a premium dog supplement brand?
- What AOV and margin structure would make paid acquisition viable?
- How important is subscription or reorder behavior?
- How quickly should CAC pay back?
- What first 90-day metrics would you watch?
- What would tell you to scale, pause, or change strategy?

### Topic 6B: Minimum Viable Testing Budget

The call should produce a practical view on the minimum budget required to learn something reliable. Broono should distinguish between a budget that can technically run ads and a budget that can generate enough signal to make decisions.

Questions:

- What is the minimum viable monthly budget to test Meta properly for a brand like Broono?
- Below what budget would the data be too noisy to trust?
- How would you allocate the first testing budget across prospecting, retargeting, creative tests, and landing page tests?
- How many creative concepts can be tested meaningfully at that budget?
- How many weeks should the initial test run before judging results?
- What daily spend is too low for Meta to exit learning or produce useful signal?
- Should the first budget optimize for purchases, quiz completions, email signups, add-to-cart, or landing page engagement?
- How many conversions or qualified actions are needed before we can call a result meaningful?
- What would be your recommended 30-day, 60-day, and 90-day test budget?
- If Broono only had a conservative test budget, what would you cut first: number of audiences, number of creatives, number of landing pages, or number of channels?

Decision we want from this section:

> What is the smallest budget that lets Broono make a real decision, not just collect impressions and ambiguous data?

### Topic 7: Lessons From Gruns

Questions:

- What were the biggest non-obvious lessons from Gruns?
- What did Meta contribute to the outcome?
- What creative or funnel insights were most important?
- How much of the growth came from retention and habit formation?
- What mistakes did Gruns avoid that other supplement brands make?
- What would you copy from that playbook for Broono?
- What would you explicitly not copy?

## 6. Broono's Working Hypotheses To Pressure-Test

Broono should go into the conversation with hypotheses, not fixed conclusions.

### Hypothesis 1: Meta is a learning channel before it is a scale channel.

Meta should reveal which messages, hooks, formats, and landing paths create qualified intent. Scaling comes only after creative and economics are proven.

### Hypothesis 2: Broono needs both education and conversion paths.

The brand idea of healthspan needs explanation. But performance marketing cannot become an essay. The funnel likely needs separate pathways for cold education, symptom-aware buyers, and warm retargeting.

### Hypothesis 3: Creative volume matters, but brand discipline matters more.

Broono will need enough creative variation to learn quickly. However, the brand should not chase low-trust UGC tropes, exaggerated claims, or emotional pressure tactics.

### Hypothesis 4: The website is part of acquisition, not only brand presentation.

The new site must support paid traffic with clear landing paths, fast comprehension, strong trust signals, email capture, quiz logic, PDP clarity, and product education.

### Hypothesis 5: Retention determines how aggressive Broono can be on acquisition.

If repeat purchase, subscription, and daily habit formation are strong, Broono can afford higher CAC. If retention is uncertain, paid media should stay in controlled learning mode.

### Hypothesis 6: Broono's long-term moat is authority, not only ads.

Paid media can create demand, but the durable system should include SEO, GEO, ingredient education, founder/lab credibility, email education, customer stories, and creator proof.

## 8. Recommended Closing Question

End the call with a forcing question:

> "If you were advising Broono for the next 90 days, and your goal was not to spend the most money but to learn the fastest without damaging the brand, what would you do first, what would you measure, and what would you refuse to do?"

This should reveal whether their approach is disciplined, brand-aware, and commercially grounded.

The strongest answer will likely connect five things: creative testing, landing page strategy, acquisition economics, retention/habit formation, and trust-building content. If the answer is mainly "run more ads," it is probably too shallow for Broono.
