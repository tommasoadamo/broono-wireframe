## 1. What We Are Selling

### 1.1 Brand Essence & Mission

**Brand essence:** Protect the Years That Matter.

**Mission:** We build daily rituals that protect a lifetime of love.

**Core belief:** Dog care should begin before something goes wrong. Dogs are not just companions — they are part of the rhythm of our lives, steadying us, grounding us, witnessing us. Their years are precious. So we protect them — daily, intentionally, beautifully.

### 1.2 Category Position

Broono is a **modern healthspan house for dogs** — devoted to extending the vibrant years through daily, preventative care with calm confidence and uncompromising craft.

We are building a category within the dog supplement space: **healthspan-first canine care**. Modern ritual meets quiet science.

**We are NOT:**

- The pharmacy aisle
- The Amazon "fix-it-fast" / "me-too" supplement shelf
- The problem-only brand ("hips," "itching," "anxiety")

**We are:**

- The daily baseline for dog parents who think long-term
- The brand that makes *healthspan* feel natural in dog care
- A practice, not just a product — a house built on devotion

Our ambition is cultural: shift behaviour **from reaction to ritual**. So, over time, people don't reach for "a supplement," they reach for a **healthspan practice**.

> Quality check for every asset: *Does this feel like a house built around devotion — or a company selling chews?*

### 1.3 Product Category

Premium dog wellness supplements — soft chews designed for daily use. Not medicine, not treats — a daily healthspan ritual.

### 1.4 Product Lines

| Product Name | Benefit Area             | Core Promise                                            |
| ------------ | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Move         | Joint health & comfort   | Support flexible movement and joint comfort as dogs age |
| Prebiotic    | Digestive wellness       | Promote healthy digestion and nutrient absorption       |
| Calm         | Calm & emotional balance | Help promote relaxation and calmer days                 |
| Essential    | Energy & longevity       | Support overall energy and long-term wellbeing          |

### 1.5 Product Format

Soft chews — approachable, easy to administer, designed to integrate into daily routines as a ritual rather than a medical intervention. Each chew contains a precise, science-backed formulation of natural ingredients.

### 1.6 Key Differentiators

- **Science-backed formulations** — developed with rigorous research, not marketing claims
- **Natural ingredients** — carefully selected, transparently communicated with full dosages
- **Italian-made** — produced in Italian labs; Italian design heritage throughout
- **Healthspan-first positioning** — preventative daily ritual, not a reactive fix
- **Premium quality** — ingredient sourcing, formulation, packaging, and brand experience all reflect a premium tier

### 1.7 Price Positioning

Premium. The website must communicate quality, trust, and value at every touchpoint — the visitor should never question *why* the price is what it is.

### 1.8 Target Audience

Modern, health-conscious pet parents (25–55) who:

- Treat their dog as family
- Value prevention over reactive treatment
- Appreciate design, quality, and transparency
- Are willing to invest in long-term wellbeing
- Are sceptical of miracle claims and marketing hype

## 2. Brand Personality

> Sources: `0-instructions/broono-brand-manual-v01.md` §1–2, `0-instructions/brand-guidelines.md`

### 2.1 Positioning Statement

Broono is a modern healthspan house for dogs, devoted to extending the vibrant years through daily, preventative care — with calm confidence and uncompromising craft. We are not "selling chews." We are offering stewardship.

### 2.2 Archetype — The Sage

Broono is **The Sage**: a generous, science-grounded mentor who shares wisdom with kindness. The Sage earns trust through evidence and clarity, not persuasion tricks or emotional manipulation. It pursues truth, competence, authority, transparency, and a genuine educational drive.

The Sage is the mentor and guide who offers reassurance through objective knowledge, clarity, and concrete solutions: it does not promise miracles, but conscious choices and advice that improve life.

### 2.3 Emotional Territory

Broono is built on science and truth, not fear.

**We stand for:**

- Ritual
- Protection
- Continuity
- Devotion
- Modern responsibility
- A beautiful life lived well (and longer)

**We avoid:**

- Panic
- Guilt
- "Before it's too late" messaging
- Dramatic decline narratives

### 2.4 Personality Keywords

| Keyword     | Weight  |
| ----------- | ------- |
| Premium     | Primary |
| Trustworthy | Primary |
| Scientific  | Primary |
| Modern      | Primary |
| Italian     | Primary |
| Emotional   | Primary |

### 2.5 Sage Traits Applied to Web Design

| Trait                       | How It Shows on the Website                                                                                              |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Truth-seeking               | Transparent ingredient lists with exact dosages, sourcing info. No vague claims. Every statement can be verified.        |

## 3. Brand Voice

> Sources: `0-instructions/broono-brand-manual-v01.md` §2.3, `0-instructions/brand-guidelines.md`

### 3.1 Tone Essence

- Calm
- Intentional
- Premium but human
- Intelligent but not clinical
- Poetic but never sentimental
- Minimal

> **A healthspan house speaks softly because it's certain.**

### 3.2 Tone Pillars

| Pillar                                | Application on Website                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Competent and reassuring**    | Expert yet accessible. Clear communication without jargon walls. Every message grounded in scientific research. Trust through knowledge.           |
| **Kind and constructive**       | Caring guidance, not paternalism. Useful advice and practical guidance. "We're in this together" energy. Caring for an animal is a shared journey. |
| **Non-imposing and guiding**    | Language that suggests, invites reflection, and encourages choice. No commands, no countdown timers, no false urgency. No unnecessary pressure.    |
| **Educational and transparent** | Speaks sincerely about both limits and potential without exaggeration. Tone of someone who wants to educate, not sell at any cost.                 |

### 3.3 Copywriting Template

| Layer                    | Role         | Example                                                              |
| ------------------------ | ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Headline**       | Emotion      | "Protect the Years That Matter."                                     |
| **Body copy**      | Clarity      | Plain-language explainer with concrete benefits, conservative claims |
| **Footnote / FAQ** | Transparency | What it does, what it doesn't, honest expectations                   |

> Rule: Emotion in headlines. Clarity in the body copy. Poetic outside. Rational inside.

Pair one poetic headline with one plain-language explainer line. Make benefits concrete; keep claims conservative and compliant. Avoid exaggerated outcomes or "miracle" framing.

### 3.4 Tagline System

| Type                      | Line                                           |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **Primary tagline** | Protect the Years That Matter.                 |
| Secondary                 | Devotion, made daily.                          |
| Secondary                 | Preventative care for long, vibrant years.     |
| Secondary                 | Extend the Good Years.                         |
| Secondary                 | A daily ritual for long, vibrant years.        |
| Secondary                 | Healthspan, for the ones who hold us together. |
| Secondary                 | Built for a lifetime of mornings.              |

**Psychological hook** (use sparingly — for campaigns and brand films, not repeated everywhere):

> *The daily ritual that protects your quiet protector.*

### 3.5 Voice Do's and Don'ts

| Don't                                | Do                                                                                      |
| ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "This supplement cures joint pain"   | "This supplement helps support joint comfort"                                           |
| "Instant calm for anxious dogs."     | "Helps promote relaxation and calmer days, especially when used regularly."             |
| "Miracle formula for your dog."      | "A science-backed formula designed to support your dog's long-term wellbeing."          |
| "Guaranteed results in a few days."  | "Many pet parents start to notice changes over time when used consistently."            |
| "You need this product."             | "This may be a helpful option if you're looking to…"                                   |
| "The best supplement on the market." | "Thoughtfully crafted with carefully selected natural ingredients, based on science."   |
| "Don't miss this crazy offer"        | "Thinking about a new wellness habit for your dog?…"                                   |
| "All dogs love these chews."         | "Many dogs enjoy these chews as part of their daily routine."                           |
| "Stop wasting time on other brands." | "If you value science-backed, natural supplements, Broono is crafted with you in mind." |

### 3.6 CTA Language Guidelines

CTAs should feel like invitations, not commands. They reflect the non-imposing, guiding nature of the Sage — encouraging choice rather than demanding action.

| Context                   | Example CTA                                   |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Home page hero            | "Start the Ritual."                           |
| Product page — primary   | "Start the habit" / "Add to routine"          |
| Product page — secondary | "Explore ingredients" / "Learn how it works"  |
| Collection / discovery    | "Discover the range" / "Meet the supplements" |
| Blog / educational        | "Keep reading" / "Learn more"                 |
| Cart upsell               | "Complete the routine"                        |
| Newsletter                | "Stay curious"                                |

## 4. Design References

### 4.1 Reference Materials

Design references, moodboards, and competitor benchmarks are stored in:

```
0-instructions/0.0 design-references/
```

Each reference should include a short annotation explaining what specifically to draw from it (layout pattern, colour usage, typography treatment, photography style, etc.).

### 4.2 Design Philosophy

The website should feel like a **premium Italian wellness brand that happens to be for dogs** — not a pet store. The visitor's first impression should be of quality, clarity, and warmth — the "dog" part is the subject, not the aesthetic driver.

Design cues should be pulled from:

- High-end human wellness / supplement brands (Ritual, Athletic Greens, Seed)
- Italian design heritage (clean geometry, editorial confidence, purposeful restraint)
- Modern editorial magazines (generous whitespace, strong type hierarchy, photography-led storytelling)

### 4.3 Design Principles

| Principle                           | Meaning                                                                                                                      |
| ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Calm confidence**           | The layout breathes. No visual clutter. Quality speaks through space and restraint, not decoration.                          |
| **Science meets warmth**      | Rigorous information presented with approachable, human design. Data wrapped in emotion. Facts delivered with care.          |
| **Editorial, not e-commerce** | Sections feel like curated editorial spreads, not generic product grids. Each page tells a story.                            |
| **Photography-first**         | Imagery does the heavy lifting. Text supports, doesn't compete. The visual impression anchors every section.                 |
| **Italian simplicity**        | Every element earns its place. If it doesn't serve a purpose, it doesn't exist. Restraint is the highest form of confidence. |

### 4.4 Brand Behaviour on the Web

A healthspan house speaks softly because it's certain. These behavioural guidelines shape every page and asset:

**We choose:**

- Fewer, stronger claims
- More whitespace
- More conviction
- Slower pacing in video
- Calm music or minimal sound design
- No overexcited edits, no chaotic hook-farming

**We don't do:**

- Loud benefit stacks
- "3 days to fix X" energy
- Before/after drama
- Sick-dog imagery as the primary aesthetic

> Every touchpoint should show ritual and continuity, not crisis.

| Level      | Size (approx.) | Notes                                                               |
| ---------- | -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| H1         | 48–64 px      | Must still feel impactful — Pesto M. is designed for large display |
| H2         | 32–40 px      |                                                                     |
| H3         | 24–28 px      |                                                                     |
| Body       | 16–18 px      | Minimum 16 px for readability                                       |
| Body small | 14 px          | Never below 14 px                                                   |

### 5.4 Quote Hierarchy

| Element        | Typeface             | Size  |
| -------------- | -------------------- | ----- |
| Short title    | Pesto M. Regular     | 60 pt |
| Core quote     | Apfel Grotezk Fett   | 24 pt |
| Quote          | Apfel Grotezk Mittel | 24 pt |
| Extended quote | Apfel Grotezk Mittel | 20 pt |
| Signature      | The Doctor Regular   | 72 pt |

Quotes follow a structured hierarchy channelling the Sage archetype: bold titles draw attention, nuanced bodies educate calmly, and handwritten signatures add human warmth.

### 5.5 Typography Rules

- **Pesto M.** is reserved for headlines and brand statements only — never for body text or UI elements
- **Apfel Grotezk Fett** (700) for subheadings, buttons, and emphasis
- **Apfel Grotezk Mittel** (500) for paragraph text and supporting copy
- **Apfel Grotezk Regular** (400) for body text and general reading
- **The Doctor** is used exclusively for signature taglines and accent moments — sparingly, never for long text
- Letter-spacing: normal across the board (Pesto and Apfel are designed with built-in spacing)
- Text transform: avoid ALL CAPS except for very short labels (e.g., "NEW", category tags)
- Maximum line length for body text: ~70 characters for optimal readability
- Hierarchy must be clear on every page — the reader should always know what's most important

## 7. Design System — Spacing & Layout

### 7.1 Spacing Scale

Use an 8 px base grid. All spacing values should be multiples of 8.

| Token   | Value  | Usage                                       |
| ------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| `xs`  | 8 px   | Tight gaps (icon-to-label, inline elements) |
| `sm`  | 16 px  | Compact spacing (within cards, form fields) |
| `md`  | 24 px  | Standard element spacing                    |
| `lg`  | 32 px  | Section internal padding                    |
| `xl`  | 48 px  | Between subsections                         |
| `2xl` | 64 px  | Between major sections (mobile)             |
| `3xl` | 96 px  | Between major sections (desktop)            |
| `4xl` | 128 px | Hero section padding (desktop)              |

### 7.2 Layout Grid

| Breakpoint            | Columns | Gutter | Margin                     |
| --------------------- | ------- | ------ | -------------------------- |
| Mobile (< 768 px)     | 4       | 16 px  | 16 px                      |
| Tablet (768–1199 px) | 8       | 24 px  | 32 px                      |
| Desktop (≥ 1200 px)  | 12      | 32 px  | auto (max-width container) |

### 7.3 Container Widths

| Container | Max Width | Usage                                                    |
| --------- | --------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Narrow    | 720 px    | Blog articles, text-heavy pages, focused reading         |
| Standard  | 1200 px   | Default page content, product pages, grids               |
| Wide      | 1440 px   | Hero sections, full-bleed imagery                        |
| Full      | 100%      | Edge-to-edge backgrounds with content constrained inside |

### 7.4 Section Rhythm

Sections should alternate between visual weight and breathing room. The overall vertical rhythm should feel **unhurried and editorial** — generous padding between sections, no cramped stacking. Alternate between content-dense and spacious sections to create a natural reading flow.

## 8. Photography Requirements

### 8.1 Shot Categories

#### A. Ingredient Photography

Isolated, levitating ingredients against pure white backgrounds. Elevates natural components as heroes of proactive wellness. These images are a core brand asset used across every touchpoint.

| Attribute                 | Specification                                                   |
| ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Style**           | Isolated, levitating ingredients against pure white backgrounds |
| **Lighting**        | Chiaroscuro — dramatic depth, high-end realism                 |
| **Aspect ratios**   | 1:1 (product cards, ingredient grid), 4:3 (detail views)        |
| **Use**             | Product pages, ingredient glossary, social close-ups, packaging |
| **Quantity needed** | One hero shot per ingredient used across all products           |

**Midjourney prompt template:**

> Limbo image of **[ingredient name]** on a white background, levitating through air, chiaroscuro effects, high-end product photography style, natural color grading, 100mm lens, f/4, ISO 100, high dynamic range, cinematic lighting, photorealistic perfection, clean

#### B. Lifestyle Photography

Pet parents and/or dogs in natural, joyful routines — playing, relaxing, or sharing quiet moments. These images position supplements as part of an aspirational everyday wellness lifestyle.

| Attribute               | Specification                                                                        |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Style**         | Pet parents and/or dogs in natural, joyful routines                                  |
| **Lighting**      | Soft, warm, natural light                                                            |
| **Settings**      | Premium interiors, outdoor natural scenes — elegance meets vitality                 |
| **Emotion**       | Authentic connection, genuine moments — never staged                                |
| **Aspect ratios** | 16:9 (hero banners), 3:2 (editorial sections), 1:1 (grid/social), 9:16 (mobile hero) |
| **Use**           | Home page hero, about sections, blog headers, social media                           |

**Rules:**

- No staged poses or people looking directly at camera
- Genuine connection between dog and pet parent
- Must feel aspirational yet attainable
- Premium settings but not sterile

#### C. Product Photography

Clean, professional product shots that communicate premium quality. The pack and chew must look as good as any high-end human wellness product.

| Attribute                             | Specification                                                                                                                |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Style**                       | Clean, studio-lit product shots — pack and chew                                                                             |
| **Lighting**                    | Even, bright, premium — consistent across all SKUs                                                                          |
| **Backgrounds**                 | Pure white (e-commerce), brand colour (lifestyle context), or transparent (compositing)                                      |
| **Aspect ratios**               | 1:1 (product cards, thumbnails), 4:5 (product page hero), 3:2 (editorial)                                                    |
| **Use**                         | Product page gallery, collection grid, cart, upsell modules                                                                  |

#### D. Editorial / Brand Photography

Italian-inspired editorial imagery that reinforces Broono's design heritage and premium positioning. Used for storytelling moments across the site.

| Attribute               | Specification                                                          |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Style**         | Italian-inspired editorial — think design magazine, not pet catalogue |
| **Aspect ratios** | Variable — match section layout needs                                 |
| **Use**           | Blog headers, about page, brand story sections                         |

### 8.2 Visual Content Direction

Photography and video should show ritual and continuity, not crisis.

**"Daily rituals" looks like:**

- Hands opening the jar
- Bowls, water, morning light
- Repetition, calm consistency
- Close-ups of texture, craft, restraint

**"Long, vibrant years" looks like:**

- Movement, vitality, calm strength
- Dogs in their element (walks, play, recovery, ease)
- No "sick dog" framing, no dramatic rescue tone

**Texture references (aesthetic, not imitation):**

- Soft light, quiet luxury, Italian restraint
- Handwritten notes / napkin philosophy (minimal, imperfect)
- Close-up intimacy: hands in fur, collar tags, leash clips, quiet moments

### 8.3 Photography Don'ts

- No stock photography look — everything must feel curated and intentional
- No cluttered backgrounds or busy compositions
- No harsh flash or flat lighting
- No dogs in costumes or anthropomorphised scenarios
- No overly clinical / veterinary aesthetics
- No low-resolution or poorly lit images — quality is non-negotiable
- No sick-dog imagery as the primary aesthetic
- No dramatic decline narratives in visual storytelling

## 9. What We Don't Want

### 9.1 Design Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern                                                  | Why                                                                           |
| ------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Generic pet store aesthetic                                   | Broono is a wellness brand that happens to be for dogs, not a pet shop        |
| Cluttered layouts with too many elements competing            | Violates calm Sage personality and Italian design simplicity                  |
| Stock photography look                                        | Destroys premium positioning and trust                                        |
| Bright neon colours or garish gradients                       | Off-brand; violates HSL guardrails                                            |
| Heavy drop shadows, bevels, or glossy effects                 | Dated, fights the clean modern aesthetic                                      |
| Carousel/slider overload                                      | Poor UX, often ignored; use intentional single-image hero or editorial scroll |
| Popup spam (exit-intent, newsletter on load, multiple modals) | Contradicts non-imposing Sage voice                                           |
| Countdown timers, fake urgency, "Only X left!"                | Fundamentally at odds with brand values — truth over miracles                |
| Tiny text on large whitespace (wasteful minimalism)           | Clean ≠ empty. Whitespace must feel purposeful, not lazy.                    |
| Walls of text with no visual hierarchy                        | Educational doesn't mean text-heavy — break info into digestible pieces      |

### 9.2 UX Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern                              | Why                                                   |
| ----------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Auto-playing video with sound             | Hostile UX                                            |
| Infinite scroll without clear navigation  | Disorienting for educational content                  |
| Hidden navigation or mystery-meat icons   | The Sage guides clearly — navigation must be obvious |
| Aggressive upselling at every turn        | Contradicts "non-imposing and guiding" voice          |
| Required account creation before purchase | Friction that kills conversion                        |
| Chat bots popping up unprompted           | Interrupts the calm, editorial experience             |

### 9.3 Copy Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern                                        | Why                                                                        |
| --------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Miracle claims or cure language                     | Violates Sage truth-seeking; potential regulatory issues                   |
| Superlatives ("best", "greatest", "#1")             | Unsubstantiated; erodes trust                                              |
| Fear-based marketing ("Your dog is suffering…")    | Manipulative; contradicts kind, constructive tone                          |
| Panic, guilt, or "before it's too late" messaging   | Fundamentally at odds with emotional territory — we protect, not frighten |
| Dramatic decline narratives                         | We show vitality and continuity, not crisis                                |
| Loud benefit stacks                                 | A healthspan house speaks softly because it's certain                      |
| "3 days to fix X" energy                            | We are building a practice, not selling a quick fix                        |

## 11. Mobile Specifications

### 11.1 General Principles

- **Mobile-first design** — all layouts must work at 375 px width minimum
- **Touch targets** — minimum 44×44 px for all interactive elements
- **Sticky Add to Cart** — on product pages, a bottom bar with product name + price + CTA sticks on scroll
- **Thumb-zone awareness** — primary CTAs should fall in the natural thumb reach zone
- **No horizontal scroll** (except intentional carousels with clear swipe affordance)
- **Images** — serve responsive sizes via Shopify's `image_url` with appropriate `width` parameters
- **Typography** — never below 14 px for any text; body text minimum 16 px

### 14.1 Video player

**Block:** `blocks/video-player-new.liquid`
**JS component:** `assets/video-player-new.js` (`video-player-new` custom element)
**CSS:** appended to `assets/base.css`

#### Purpose

A reusable block for playing video with full merchant control over playback behaviour and loading performance. Supports Shopify-hosted uploads, self-hosted file URLs (H.265/HEVC, WebM, MP4), and external YouTube/Vimeo URLs.

#### Files

| File                               | Role                                              |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| `blocks/video-player-new.liquid` | Block markup + schema                             |
| `assets/video-player-new.js`     | `video-player-new` web component                |
| `assets/base.css`                | CSS for `video-player-new` and its sub-elements |

#### Schema settings (theme editor)

| Setting                      | Type          | Options / Notes                                                  |
| ---------------------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Source                       | Select        | Uploaded / External URL                                          |
| Video                        | Video picker  | Shopify-hosted upload (uploaded only)                            |
| Custom poster image          | Image picker  | Overrides built-in video preview (uploaded only)                 |
| External URL                 | video_url     | YouTube or Vimeo (url only)                                      |
| Cover image                  | Image picker  | Poster for external videos (url only)                            |
| Alt text                     | Text          | For external videos (url only)                                   |
| Start video                  | Select        | On click / On hover / Automatically (uploaded only)              |
| Start with audio on          | Checkbox      | Click mode only — auto/hover are always muted (browser policy)  |
| Loop                         | Checkbox      | Default: on                                                      |
| Preload                      | Select        | None / Metadata (default) / Auto (uploaded only)                 |
| Load when scrolled into view | Checkbox      | Lazy-loads via IntersectionObserver, default: on (uploaded only) |
| Width                        | Range 0–100% | Desktop                                                          |
| Width (mobile)               | Range 0–100% | Mobile                                                           |
| Aspect ratio                 | Select        | Auto / 16:9 / 1:1 / 9:16 (url only, no cover image)              |
| Border                       | Select        | None / Solid                                                     |
| Border thickness             | Range         | px                                                               |
| Border opacity               | Range         | %                                                                |
| Border radius                | Range         | px                                                               |
| Padding                      | Range ×4     | top / bottom / left / right, px                                  |

#### Trigger behaviour

| Mode          | Behaviour                                                                        | Audio                                  |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| On click      | Shows poster image with play button overlay. Clicking loads and plays the video. | Respects "Start with audio on" setting |
| On hover      | Poster fades out on `pointerenter`, video plays; pauses on `pointerleave`.   | Always muted (browser policy)          |
| Automatically | Video loads (or lazy-loads on scroll) and autoplays immediately.                 | Always muted (browser policy)          |

External URL source always uses click-to-play via the existing `deferred-media` component; trigger/audio/preload/lazy settings are hidden in the editor for external URLs.

#### Loading performance

- **Preload none** — no video data fetched until playback starts; best for page weight.
- **Preload metadata** (default) — only duration/dimensions fetched; fast seek without full download.
- **Preload auto** — browser pre-buffers the video; fastest first-play, highest bandwidth cost.
- **Lazy load** (default on) — the `<video>` element is not inserted into the DOM until the block enters the viewport (200 px rootMargin). Combine with `preload: none` for maximum bandwidth saving.

#### Multi-video coordination

The component listens for `ThemeEvents.mediaStartedPlaying` and pauses itself when any other media element starts playing, matching the theme's singleton-play behaviour.

#### Brand alignment

- Auto-play and hover modes always start muted — consistent with §9.2 "Auto-playing video with sound" anti-pattern.
- Click-to-play poster uses the existing `deferred-media__poster-icon` play icon styling for visual consistency.
- Lazy loading and `preload: metadata` defaults support the LCP < 2.5 s performance target (§11.3).
